About

I've been noticing things my whole life without knowing there was a name for it. These three stories are the most honest answer I have to why that name might be advertising.

In 8th standard, I borrowed money from a friend and bought a Cadbury Silk for a girl. I wasn't really thinking about chocolate. I was following a script that Cadbury had already written — that in India, if you love someone, you give them a Silk. The ad had done its job so completely that I wasn't even aware it had done anything. I was just a boy with borrowed money, standing outside a classroom, holding purple packaging like it was a marriage proposal. Tapping the right emotion is the backbone of any narrative.

"Darr ke aage jeet hai" was my wallpaper throughout UPSC preparation. Not because I'm a Mountain Dew loyalist but because fear is the one emotion that nobody escapes. Mountain Dew just found a way to sell a drink through it. Nike did it with laziness. Cadbury did it with love. The product is almost incidental.

While conducting field research during the 2024 Lok Sabha elections, I realised the same behaviour applies to voters. Choosing a candidate is not very different from picking a packet of chips off a rack. Every option has a USP, but only the one that taps the right emotion gets picked.
